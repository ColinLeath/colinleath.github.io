Here is a description of the camp:
  • Build a Multimedia Web Site.
  • Learn coding, podcasting, blogging, and videocasting.
  • Design your own web site.
To address that expectation, and to go beyond it, I would do two things:

(1) Begin (after icebreakers and get-to-know you exercises) by exploring participant expectations for the week and for the kind of networked experience they are most interested in creating.

(2) Challenge them to share and explore where they think network technology is going and where the content, services, or applications they create will fit in that future.

Here is a brainstorm of the areas I would prepare to cover in the curriculum (What I'll actually cover will be based on student interest):
  • Usability & accessibility
  • The future of Internet content services - (Very likely this is not "designing a web page" but designing a gadget or a content feed that users can add to their own pages, devices, and applications--see iGoogle and Google Gadgets/Desktop/Android for examples of this. Or the sdtjdph video feed slide show.)
  • Recording media: photos, audio, video
  • Editing media
  • Graphic design
  • Geolocalization - publishing content on or incorporating Google Maps, Google Earth and other mapping services
  • Localization - making applications accessible in multiple languages
  • Publishing (feed syndication)
  • Traffic measurement, visitor tracking, and navigation analysis
  • Marketing
  • Social networking (e.g., OpenSocial, Facebook, MySpace, Twitter)
  • Mashups (combination and resyndication/redisplay of feeds)
  • Programming: HTML, CSS, Javascript, AJAX, Python, etc.
  • Interface design / designing for a particular platform (platform = hardware/software combination)
  • Application design (given the features of the platform, what does the application do?)
  • Hosting your content, service, or application
  • Editing your content, service, or application
  • The creative process
  • Ethics
Guest speakers could come from established bloggers, journalists, artists, as well as Web 2.0 companies (such as the local digital-telepathy), graphic designers (such as Jonathan Louie - Point Loma resident and designer for Wired, the Mingei), and Platt College.

Please let me know if you'd like more information.
